About Me

I'm an operations leader with over 15 years of experience in regulated beverage manufacturing, spanning brewing, distillation, whiskey blending, and packaging. My career has been built on the intersection of hands-on problem solving and systems thinking. I've helped build facilities from the ground up, developed new products from scratch touching everything from recipe and brand development to packaging, distribution, and consumer events, and designed practical tools for forecasting, compliance, and cross-functional execution.

Along the way, leadership kept finding me. I've run brewing and packaging crews, stepped into a Director of Sales role managing a dispersed team and multi-state distributor relationships, and regularly found myself as the person others looked to when something needed to get figured out, even when that wasn't in the job description.

I'm currently pursuing a B.A.S. in Leadership and Management at Chemeketa, not to learn what leadership looks like in theory, but to sharpen the language and frameworks around what I've already been doing, and to close the gaps I know are there.

This portfolio is a record of that process.

Curiosity

Constantly learning and staying genuinely engaged with new people and ideas is something I work at deliberately. It drives me to keep asking why until I actually understand something, and then to turn that understanding into something useful for the people around me.

Optimism

Being willing to invest time and energy into things that won't pay off for a long time requires a genuine belief that the future is worth building toward. Most of the work I do operates on timelines measured in years, sometimes decades, and that's only sustainable if you actually believe in where you're headed.

Analytical

I gather information into a structured system, work to deeply understand how things work together, and summarize what I find into something useful for others. I've built much of my career in brewing and distilling on this strength.

Empathy

I pay attention to how someone's emotional state affects others and a group's performance, not just how it affects the individual. I tend to notice when something is off before it gets said out loud, and I try to do something about it early enough to matter.

Positivity

Enthusiasm is contagious, and I've learned to use that deliberately. Being able to lead a narrative with genuine optimism is a powerful tool, and when it's authentic it can help build successful teams through almost anything.

Intellection

I've worked to develop expertise by being very methodical about how I learn things. The pitfall is getting mired in research instead of moving into actual skill development, so I try to use that depth to raise the collective knowledge base of the team rather than keeping it to myself.

Restorative

This has been a constant guiding force in my life. Bringing creative solutions to help solve a problem is one of the skills that has helped me the most in my career, and I have a track record of recovering projects that others had written off. I'm working on applying that same diagnostic energy upstream, to the processes and communication breakdowns that allow failure to happen in the first place.

My expertise is currently rooted in alcohol manufacturing, but my real skill set is in managing complex manufacturing environments, navigating regulatory hurdles, and building data-driven systems. In the next 3–5 years, I want to solidify my ability to manage large, diverse teams as a VP of Operations or General Manager, moving beyond production oversight to focus on the human systems of a business.
