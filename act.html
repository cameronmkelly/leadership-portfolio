Act Like a Leader

Most of the leadership I've done wasn't assigned to me. It showed up as a problem that needed solving, a gap nobody else was filling, or a system that didn't exist yet. This section is a record of that work.

Project
Standard Operating Procedure Development

I've written SOPs for multiple facilities over the years and the process always had the same problem: documents end up scattered, hard to navigate, and disconnected from each other. Existing document control platforms are either too expensive for a small operation or too complicated to justify. There were no templates that actually fit how a lean facility works. So during a period when my company had some breathing room, I built one from scratch: a structured, cross-linked framework that connects SOPs to the equipment they govern, the chemicals they require, and the vendors who support them. It's designed to be the thing I wish had existed at every job I've ever had.
